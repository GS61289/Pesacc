Cognia Accreditation Standards

The Cognia accreditation process is based on a set of rigorous standards, grouped under four Key Characteristics. Click a Key Characteristic below to view its standards, responsible teams, data types, and example evidence.

Standard 1: Leaders cultivate and sustain a culture that demonstrates respect and fairness for all learners and is free from bias.

Committee: Culture of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Perception, Documentation
  • Analyzed stakeholder survey results (respect, fairness, bias)
  • Handbook sections on purpose/expectations
  • Evaluation reports of relevant programs
  • Site council minutes/membership lists

Standard 2: Learners' well-being is at the heart of the institution's guiding principles, such as mission, purpose, and beliefs.

Committee: Culture of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Perception, Documentation, Observation
  • Analyzed student survey results (support systems)
  • Handbook sections on student support
  • Classroom observation summaries
  • Mission/Vision statements

Standard 3: Leaders actively engage stakeholders to support the institution's priorities and guiding principles that promote learners' academic growth and well-being.

Committee: Culture of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Perception, Performance, Documentation
  • Stakeholder survey results (involvement, support)
  • Student performance data (priority setting)
  • School Improvement Plan
  • Policy handbook sections on stakeholder involvement

Standard 4: Learners benefit from a formal structure that fosters positive relationships with peers and adults.

Committee: Culture of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Master schedule showing advisory/support programs
  • Curriculum materials for SEL/relationship building
  • Survey results on student/staff relationships

Standard 5: Professional staff members embrace effective collegiality and collaboration in support of learners.

Committee: Culture of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • PLC plans/agendas/minutes
  • Staff survey results on PLC effectiveness
  • Program evaluation data supporting collaboration

Standard 6: Professional staff members receive the support they need to strengthen their professional practice.

Committee: Culture of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Procedures for resource requests
  • Staff surveys on resource availability/support
  • Mentoring/coaching program documentation

Standard 7: Leaders guide professional staff members in the continuous improvement process focused on learners' experiences and needs.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Performance, Perception
  • Continuous Improvement Plan document
  • Data reports showing plan implementation
  • Policy/handbook sections on stakeholder involvement
  • Meeting minutes showing staff engagement

Standard 8: The governing authority demonstrates a commitment to learners by collaborating with leaders to uphold the institution's priorities and to drive continuous improvement.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Governing authority policy manual
  • Board meeting minutes
  • Documentation of board training
  • Survey results on board collaboration

Standard 9: Leaders cultivate effective individual and collective leadership among stakeholders.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Staff/student recognition programs
  • Survey results on leadership opportunities
  • Policy/handbook sections on leadership roles

Standard 10: Leaders demonstrate expertise in recruiting, supervising, and evaluating professional staff members to optimize learning.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation
  • Staff qualification records/certifications
  • Recruitment materials
  • Supervision/evaluation instruments
  • Staff retention data

Standard 11: Leaders create and maintain institutional structures and processes that support learners and staff members in both stable and changing environments.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation
  • Emergency/crisis management plans
  • Handbooks outlining procedures
  • Communication logs/protocols
  • Org charts

Standard 12: Professional staff members implement curriculum and instruction that are aligned for relevancy and effectiveness for each and every learner.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation
  • Curriculum maps/guides
  • Instructional framework documentation
  • Observation/evaluation instruments
  • Curriculum review process documentation

Standard 13: Qualified personnel instruct and assist learners and each other in support of the institution's mission, purpose, and beliefs.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Records of staff qualifications/certifications
  • Professional development attendance records
  • Survey data on staff support/effectiveness

Standard 14: Curriculum and instruction are augmented by reliable information resources and materials that advance learning and support learners' personal interests.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Process for selecting instructional resources
  • Inventory of available resources
  • Survey data on resource adequacy

Standard 15: Learners' needs drive the allocation and management of human, material, digital, and fiscal resources.

Committee: Leadership for Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Learner needs assessment data
  • Strategic/Improvement plan sections
  • Budget documents
  • Survey data on resource allocation

Standard 16: Learners experience curriculum and instruction that emphasize the value of different cultures, backgrounds, and abilities reflective of the global community.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception, Observation
  • Documentation of DEI initiatives
  • Survey data on culture/respect
  • Curriculum examples showing integration of diverse cultures
  • Classroom observation notes

Standard 17: Learners have the support and opportunities to realize their learning potential.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Perception, Documentation, Observation
  • Survey data (student perspectives on support)
  • Differentiated instruction plans/materials
  • Individualized Learning Plans (ILPs)
  • Course catalogs/program descriptions
  • Classroom observation data

Standard 18: Learners are immersed in an environment that fosters lifelong skills including creativity, curiosity, risk taking, collaboration, and design thinking.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Perception, Documentation, Observation
  • Survey data (student perspectives on environment)
  • Documentation for advisory programs, SEL curriculum
  • Sample ILPs including non-academic skills
  • Classroom observation data on collaboration, risk-taking

Standard 19: Learners are immersed in an environment that promotes and respects student agency and responsibility for their learning.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Perception, Observation, Documentation
  • Survey data (student perspectives)
  • Classroom observation data (student voice, choice)
  • Sample ILPs showing student input
  • Examples of student work demonstrating self-monitoring

Standard 20: Learners engage in experiences that promote and develop their self-confidence and love of learning.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception, Observation
  • Information on RTI/support programs
  • Learner survey data (confidence, motivation, curiosity)
  • Classroom observation data (student engagement)

Standard 21: Instruction is characterized by high expectations and learner-centered practices.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Observation, Performance
  • Lesson plans showing high expectations
  • Classroom observation data (rigor, differentiation)
  • Learner performance data (growth, mastery)

Standard 22: Instruction is monitored and adjusted to advance and deepen individual learners' knowledge and understanding of the curriculum.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Observation, Documentation
  • Classroom observation data (formative assessment)
  • PD plan including data analysis training
  • Meeting minutes/reports showing data analysis

Standard 23: Professional staff members integrate digital resources that deepen and advance learners' engagement with instruction and stimulate their curiosity.

Committee: Engagement of Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Observation
  • Technology integration plan
  • Classroom observation data (digital resource use)
  • PD records on digital tool integration
  • Examples of student work using digital tools

Standard 24: Leaders use data and input from a variety of sources to make decisions for learners' and staff members' growth and well-being.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • Continuous Improvement Plan showing data use
  • Stakeholder survey data (perceptions of institution)
  • PD records on data use/analysis
  • Meeting minutes showing data-informed decision making

Standard 25: Leaders promote action research by professional staff members to improve their practice and advance learning.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Observation, Documentation
  • Analysis of classroom observations identifying needs
  • Comprehensive PD plan including action research
  • Analysis of intervention effectiveness
  • PLC implementation plan/minutes showing inquiry cycles

Standard 26: Leaders regularly evaluate instructional programs and organizational conditions to improve instruction and advance learning.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation
  • Curriculum documents (scope/sequence, maps)
  • Curriculum review cycle/plan/minutes
  • Program evaluation reports and analyses
  • Data showing decisions made based on evaluations

Standard 27: Learners' academic and non-academic needs are identified and effectively addressed through appropriate interventions.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Perception
  • RTI/MTSS plans and effectiveness data
  • Student needs assessment data
  • Documentation of specific interventions
  • Survey data on effectiveness of support systems

Standard 28: With support, learners pursue individual goals including the acquisition of academic and non-academic skills important for their educational futures and careers.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Performance
  • Personalized learning plans (goals/interests)
  • PD plan including goal-setting/personalization training
  • Learner-led conferences, career exploration projects
  • Student progress monitoring tools/reports

Standard 29: Understanding learners' needs and interests drives the design, delivery, application, and evaluation of professional learning.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation, Performance
  • Comprehensive PD plan (needs assessment, delivery, evaluation)
  • Analysis reports on PD effectiveness
  • Documentation showing PD topics aligned with student needs

Standard 30: Learners' progress is measured through a balanced system that includes assessment both for learning and of learning.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Documentation
  • Institutional assessment plan
  • Assessment data showing use for planning/modification
  • Examples of common assessments
  • PD information on assessment literacy/data use
  • Meeting minutes showing data discussions

Standard 31: Learners demonstrate growth in their academic performance based on valid and reliable assessments.

Committee: Growth in Learning KCWG | Data Types: Performance, Documentation
  • Longitudinal student achievement/growth data
  • Documentation of assessment validity/reliability
  • Plans and results for targeted interventions
  • Summaries/examples of communication with stakeholders
