Data

Data collection is a cornerstone of the Cognia accreditation process. This section outlines the required analyses, directory structure, responsibilities, and best practices for collecting, cleaning, and analyzing data to support accreditation standards.

Data Types Used in Cognia Accreditation

Documentation Official documents, policies, reports, handbooks, plans
Observation Classroom or environment observations, walkthroughs
Perception Surveys, interviews, focus groups, stakeholder feedback
Performance Student achievement, assessment results, program outcomes

Examples of Evidence by Data Type

Documentation
  • Handbooks (student, staff, parent)
  • School policies
  • Improvement/strategic plans
  • Meeting minutes, agendas
  • Curriculum maps/guides
  • Professional development records
  • Board meeting minutes
  • Emergency/crisis management plans
  • Org charts
  • Technology integration plans
  • Assessment plans
  • Resource inventories
Observation
  • Classroom/learning environment observation data (e.g., eleot)
  • Observer calibration/training records
  • Observation trend analysis reports
Perception
  • Survey results (students, staff, parents)
  • Survey instruments and raw responses
  • Focus group protocols, transcripts/notes
  • Interview summaries
  • Feedback forms
Performance
  • Student achievement data (standardized tests, internal assessments)
  • Disaggregated and longitudinal data
  • Program evaluation data
  • Assessment results
  • PD effectiveness reports
  • Examples of student work
  • RTI/MTSS effectiveness data

Analysis Process: 5 Key Steps

1
Understand Purpose

Clarify the goal of the analysis and what questions you are trying to answer.

2
Identify & Evaluate Data Sources

List all available data sources. Evaluate their relevance, validity, accuracy, sample size, collection methods, bias, and representation.

3
Select & Clean Data Sources

Choose the most relevant and reliable data. Clean data by removing irrelevant, inaccurate, or biased information. Specify sources (e.g., survey items, handbook pages).

4
Analyze & Synthesize Information

Identify themes, strengths, and weaknesses. Synthesize findings from multiple sources.

5
Interpret Findings, Prioritize, & Develop Theory of Action

Interpret the results, prioritize needs, and develop a theory of action (root cause analysis, if/then/so that statements).
